Recent Projects

Human-centered Threat Modeling

Human-centered threat modeling is a practice that researchers use to identify security and privacy threats to people, as well as ways to mitigate those threats. Our work systematizes the practice of human-centered threat modeling, identifying the core components of a human-centered threat modeling exercise by studying the practices of researchers in the area. We have applied this framework to study the security and privacy experiences of Pakistani immigrants to the U.S.

Motivation to use secure email

We conducted an interview study to provide a comprehensive understanding of the mindsets underlying adoption and use of secure email services, such as ProtonMail. Our results uncover that a defining reason for adopting a secure email system is to avoid surveillance from big tech companies. However, regardless of the complexity and accuracy of a person's mental model, our participants rarely send and receive encrypted emails, thus not making full use of the privacy they could obtain. These findings indicate that secure email systems could potentially find greater adoption by appealing to their privacy advantages, but privacy gains will be limited until a critical mass are able to join these systems and easily send encrypted emails to each other. Automated Certificates for User Authentication

We developed a new system, called Let's Authenticate, which enables people to login to websites using cryptographic methods rather than passwords. Our system combines elements of password managers, FIDO2, and certificates. Let’s Authenticate issues privacy-preserving certificates to users, automatically manages their credentials, and eliminates trust in third parties. We provide a detailed security and privacy analysis, an overhead analysis, and a systematic comparison of the system to a variety of alternatives using a well-known framework. We discuss how Let’s Authenticate compares to other systems, lessons learned from our design, and issues related to centralized management of authentication data. Read the paper
